Paul Petrowsky

I'm a principal product designer at Meta, based in Chicago. Working on strengthening people's ability to remediate issues across Instagram, Facebook, and Business tools. I'm also a co-founder of SNACKS, an AI-native design studio.

A little about me

My work at Meta lives inside Central Integrity & Support — the team responsible for what happens when someone reports a problem, asks for help, or gets stuck. After five years of focus, I've been able to help drive a shift from shipping the org chart, to a more unified product stack, to empowering the next generation of AI-first support experiences.

These days I'm not designing flows — I'm designing the system that designs the flows.

Outside of Meta, I co-founded SNACKS, a digital product studio I run with David Bean. SNACKS is where I get to work with founders and enterprise intrapreneurs directly — strategy, design, and engineering for ventures that need a senior team without a 50-person agency. It's active, profitable, and quietly one of the things I'm proudest of.

Before Meta and SNACKS, I spent over 12 years building a design practice at Dom & Tom — starting as their first design hire in 2010 and ending as Chief Design Officer, leading an 18-person practice across product, brand, and platform work for clients like Houghton Mifflin Harcourt, JPMorgan Chase, Bloomberg, Tyson Foods, NBC Universal, and the Alzheimer's Association.

And before that, I was in book publishing — five-plus years at Triumph Books, a Random House imprint, designing covers and interiors for what eventually added up to a few hundred titles. That stretch is where my typography habits and respect for editorial pacing come from. I never really left it behind.

Over twenty years in design, from book covers to platform architecture. I'm a fan of multi-brand systems, support-shaped problems, and dad jokes.
