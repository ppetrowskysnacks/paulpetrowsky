Architecting Meta's Support

A five year focus on Integrity and support — from individual resolution flows to the AI assistant rolling out to billions of Facebook and Instagram users. Principles, frameworks, cross-org collaboration and overall perseverance made it possible.

Principal Product Designer

2021 — Present

A five year focus on Integrity and support — from individual resolution flows to the AI assistant rolling out to billions of Facebook and Instagram users. Principles, frameworks, cross-org collaboration and overall perseverance made it possible.

I joined Meta in October 2021 as a Product Designer in support. Almost five years in, the work has grown from designing individual flows to designing the system that designs them — the architecture behind Meta's resolution layer, which compounded into the Unified Support Home and the Meta AI Support Assistant now rolling out globally on Facebook and Instagram.

The work compounds: each pattern made the next faster to design; each cross-org alignment unblocked the next workstream.

The journey had a shape: hard guidelines and playbooks first, then governance frameworks, then the patterns codified in the product itself. Each layer of formalization earned the next — and that progression is what made the leapfrog to AI-first support possible. When the resolution patterns are already in code, an AI assistant doesn't bolt onto features. It orchestrates the system.

Here's how it got built — one layer at a time, across three eras.

Act 1 The framework · 2022 — 2023

A playbook, not a feature.

Advertiser support was a patchwork. Same problem, three different flows depending on the surface. So I built a pattern framework — not a feature. It wasn't something users saw; it was a playbook other designers could use to make the same problem look the same everywhere.

I shipped the first two implementations: a high-volume mobile resolution surface that drove a stat-sig increase in users self-resolving and reached 100% rollout, and the ad-rejection resolution experience that ships in production across the advertiser surface today. Stat-sig wins on both active-advertiser and edit-approval metrics.

Alongside the framework, I shipped a supporting UI library and co-authored content guidelines for how Meta talks to advertisers about enforcement decisions.

You can't ship the framework. You ship the implementations that prove it.
Act 2 Scaling cross-org · 2024 — 2025

Driving consistency through a unified product stack.

By 2024, Meta had multiple resolution architectures running in parallel — for ads enforcement, organic content, subscription products, account recovery. Individually defensible. Collectively chaotic.

I co-led the unified cross-product resolution architecture: secured alignment with engineering and product leadership across the support and integrity orgs, landed it on multiple cross-org roadmaps, then drove it through a cross-org experimentation program that validated the design pattern across surfaces and use cases. The qualitative outcome: a meaningful business unlock and a shared resolution pattern other teams could reuse.

The flagship: Unified Support Home

The capstone was the Unified Support Home — Meta's centralized support hub for Facebook and Instagram, announced publicly in December 2025 and now rolling out globally on iOS and Android. The hub consolidated multiple legacy support experiences into one.

I co-led strategy and cross-org alignment, drove the executive-alignment deck that secured partnership with the Brand and Facebook teams, and integrated hacked-account recovery as a launch-milestone entry point. At a critical moment, the Facebook team brought me in to help shape the experience direction — the kind of cross-org pull I'm proudest of building, because it doesn't show up in any single artifact.

Within the support hub, you can now report an account issue, find quick answers with the help of Meta AI-powered search, and more. Meta Newsroom

The "and more" is the entire architecture. The hub isn't a feature — it's the infrastructure that makes Act 3 possible.

Act 3 AI-native consolidation · 2025 — 2026

The AI moment had arrived. The architecture absorbed it.

The Unified Support Home gave Meta a unified support place. The experience inside was still mostly traditional — search, FAQs, contact forms. The AI moment had arrived; the architecture hadn't absorbed it yet.

But here's the leapfrog: the resolution patterns from Acts 1 and 2 weren't just documented anymore — they were governance frameworks and code in the product. An AI assistant didn't need to invent the resolution layer. It needed to learn to orchestrate the one that already existed.

I led the design expansion of the Meta AI Support Assistant across Meta's advertiser support surfaces — Business Help Center and Business Support Home. For Business Help Center I designed the global assistant entry point, contextual entries on top-visited support articles (failed payments, account access, ad rejections), and an "Ask Meta AI" text-selection entry point that routes any question straight into the assistant.

The new Meta AI support assistant is designed to help resolve account problems for you from start to finish… It can respond to requests in under five seconds. Meta Newsroom

That five-second, end-to-end action capability is the architecture I'd been building since the original pattern framework in 2022, finally given the AI layer that ties it together.

Operating at scale: as design lead for in-product support, I drove the experimentation strategy that significantly exceeded the org's annual business-impact targets across dozens of validated experiments. Alongside, I led regulatory-compliance design across multiple advertiser experiences (meaningful business-risk protection), established a design-governance program that scaled review and onboarding across integrator teams, and helped found a design-quality program for the org. I mentored designers across the team and supplemented manager capacity across time zones.

I also co-led the conceptual layer that lets others make these calls consistently: principles for how Meta deploys AI in support, vision sprints that set the direction for AI-native support, and the consolidation strategy reshaping the support roadmap going forward.

Reflection What I'm proudest of

It's not the headline number. It's the architecture story.

The pattern playbooks I shipped in 2022 became the governance frameworks adopted cross-org in 2024. Those frameworks became code in the cross-product resolution architecture, and that code became the substrate an AI assistant could orchestrate in 2026.

Guidelines → governance → code → AI-first. Each layer earned the next.

I plant seeds and tend them as they grow. That's a 4.8-year story, not a single-quarter story.

What's next Growth & the next bet

Looking for the next architecture problem at this scope.

I'm looking for the next architecture problem at this scope — somewhere I can plant seeds, tend them, and have them compound for 4–5 years. Support is one substrate. Identity and onboarding are others. Anywhere the system matters more than any individual flow.
